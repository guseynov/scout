# Product

## Register

product

## Users

- Deal browsers who want to find worthwhile products quickly, compare options, and share good finds with others.
- Deal contributors and moderators who keep the collection useful by adding, reviewing, and maintaining listings.

## Product Purpose

Common Good helps a community surface useful, well-priced products without ad-auction noise or clutter. The core job is to browse, filter, preview, and share deals with confidence.

Success means people find relevant items quickly, trust the shortlist, and do not need extra tabs or detours to make a decision.

## Brand Personality

Calm, selective, trustworthy, practical.

The voice should feel concise and editorial without being precious. The interface should read like a thoughtful shortlist from someone who knows what matters.

## Anti-references

- Coupon-wall and affiliate-spam marketplaces with crowded promos, urgency banners, and noisy upsells.
- Search-result pages that bury relevance under ads, badges, and repetitive listings.
- Dark-pattern commerce flows that make filtering, previewing, or returning to the collection feel sticky.
- Overdecorated UI chrome or gimmicky motion that competes with the products themselves.

## Design Principles

- Curate, don't overwhelm.
- Familiar controls should disappear into the task.
- Community trust must be visible in the hierarchy and copy.
- Browsing should stay fast, reversible, and low-friction.
- Empty, loading, and error states should help the user keep going.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA contrast and keyboard operability.
- Do not rely on color alone for selection or status.
- Support reduced motion with minimal transitions.
- Preserve readable text at small sizes and across responsive layouts.
- Keep filters, previews, and clear actions reachable without a mouse.
